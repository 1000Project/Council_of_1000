It is not a government in the traditional sense.

The Council exists simultaneously as:

- a **physical assembly**,  
- a **metaphysical construct**,

Each of its **1000 Seats** is occupied by a Champion whose conviction is powerful enough to affect the structure of the Realms.

This document defines the public-facing explanation of the Council, its Seats, its laws, its conflicts, and its role in the TCG.

# üúÅ The Structure of the Council

## 1. The Thousand Seats  
There are **exactly 1000 Seats**, each representing a unique philosophical domain or conceptual authority.

A Seat is not a chair ‚Äî it is a **metaphysical locus of influence**, tied to:

- a belief,  
- a discipline,  
- a law of existence,  
- or a Realm-specific truth.

Examples (not canonical cards yet):

- *Seat of the Iron Mandate*  
- *Seat of Silent Memory*  
- *Seat of the Living Forge*  
- *Seat of Ruined Prophecy*  
- *Seat of the Infinite Loop*  

A Champion occupies a Seat when their power, mastery, and ideology resonate with that domain‚Äôs nature.

No two Seats are identical.  
No Seat is ever truly empty ‚Äî only **unclaimed**.

---

## 2. The High Assembly  
The highest echelon within the Council ‚Äî the collective influence of all 1000 Seats.

The High Assembly:

- votes on metaphysical decisions  
- arbitrates Realm-boundary disputes  
- sanctions or prohibits metaphysical experiments  
- maintains the fragile stability of the Thousandfold Realm  

But because ideals conflict, the Council rarely reaches consensus.  
This instability drives narrative arcs and expansion themes.

---

## 3. Realm Delegations  
Although Champions align with **one Realm only**, Realms form informal blocs:

- **Dominion Delegation:** strength, conquest, political pressure  
- **Solace Delegation:** defense, protection, preservation of stability  
- **Veil Delegation:** secrecy, espionage, knowledge control  
- **Genesis Delegation:** creation, innovation, societal engineering  
- **Ruin Delegation:** decay, inevitability, cycles of collapse  
- **Ascendance Delegation:** mastery, enlightenment, philosophical refinement  
- **Paradox Delegation:** chaos, probability, destabilization  

These blocs influence Council politics but do not control it.

# üúÉ How Seats Are Won, Lost, and Contested

Seats are not inherited.  
They are **earned**, **won**, or **torn away**.

---

## 1. Challenge Rights  
A Champion may challenge another for their Seat if:

- their philosophies directly oppose  
- their power resonates with the Seat‚Äôs nature  
- they have achieved an ascension milestone  
- the Council sanctions the challenge  
- a Realm imbalance threatens the stability of the Threshold  

Challenges can be:

- duels,  
- debates,  
- trials,  
- paradox gambits,  
- or metaphysical contests unique to the Seat.

The outcome reshapes lore and can be tied to expansion cycles.
