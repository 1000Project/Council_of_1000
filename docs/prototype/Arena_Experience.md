# Council of 1000 – Arena Experience
### Prototype Visual and Interaction Design

---

# 🜂 Purpose

The Arena Experience defines how combat feels visually and emotionally during the prototype battle.

This document ensures the visual presentation supports gameplay instead of distracting from it.

The arena should feel:

Mysterious  
Powerful  
Ancient  
Controlled  
Intentional  

The goal is atmosphere without complexity.

---

# 🜁 Visual Philosophy

The prototype should follow:

**Clarity over spectacle.**

Combat must always be readable.

Players should always understand:

Who acted  
What happened  
How much damage occurred  
Whose turn it is  

Visuals should enhance understanding, not obscure it.

---

# 🜄 Arena Tone Direction

Visual inspiration direction:

Wizard101 Arcana environments  
Raid Shadow Legends presentation  
Ancient council chambers  
Arcane summoning circles  
Mythic battlegrounds  

The arena should feel like:

A proving ground.

Not a battlefield.

This reinforces Council identity.

---

# 🜃 Arena Layout Structure

Recommended structure:

Player Champion (left)

Enemy Champion (right)

Center:

Combat space.

Top:

Turn indicator.

Bottom:

Ability bar.

Side:

Combat log.

Example layout:

TURN INDICATOR

PLAYER        ENEMY

[ Champion ]  [ Enemy ]

Arena Floor

Ability Bar

Combat Log

---

# Background Style

Prototype background options:

Stone arena floor.

Arcane circle.

Floating platform.

Energy chamber.

Council chamber.

Prototype may use:

Static image.

Animated background optional.

Do not delay MVP for visuals.

---

# Champion Presentation

Champions should appear as:

Card representation OR
Character portrait OR
Simple animated figure.

Prototype acceptable:

Card frame with image.

Name displayed.

Health bar above.

Example:

Champion Card
Name
Health Bar

Simple is fine.

---

# Health Bar Design

Health bars must:

Clearly show remaining health.

Update smoothly.

Use clear colors.

Example:

Green = healthy.

Yellow = mid.

Red = low.

Health values should be visible.

Example:

32 / 40

---

# Damage Feedback

When damage occurs:

Display damage number.

Example:

-8

Number floats briefly.

Health bar decreases.

Optional:

Brief flash effect.

Keep simple.

---

# Ability Activation Feedback

When ability used:

Ability card highlights.

Small glow effect acceptable.

Combat log updates.

Optional:

Simple particle effect.

Do not require animation assets.

---

# Turn Transition

Turn transitions should feel clear.

Example:

Turn 2 appears.

Small fade.

Combat log updates.

Optional:

Turn banner.

Example:

"Turn 3"

Clarity is more important than style.

---

# Ability Bar Design

Ability buttons should show:

Ability name.

Optional icon.

Simple description tooltip optional.

Example:

[ Blade Storm ]

[ Guardian Field ]

[ Resolve ]

Buttons should:

Disable after use if needed.

Highlight on hover or tap.

Mobile friendly.

---

# Combat Log Design

Combat log increases immersion.

Should show:

Player action.

Enemy action.

Health changes.

Example:

Turn 1:

Blade Storm deals 8 damage.

Enemy attacks for 6 damage.

Turn 2:

Guardian Field activated.

Enemy deals 3 damage.

Log should scroll.

Newest action visible.

---

# Animation Philosophy

Prototype animation should remain:

Minimal.

Acceptable:

Fade transitions.

Health bar movement.

Damage numbers.

Ability glow.

Unnecessary:

Character motion capture.

3D movement.

Complex FX.

Fun comes from interaction first.

---

# Player Interaction Feel

Player should always feel:

In control.

Interaction should feel:

Responsive.

Fast.

Clear.

Avoid:

Delays longer than 1 second.

---

# Arena Emotional Targets

Arena should create:

Anticipation.

Focus.

Momentum.

Victory satisfaction.

Not confusion.

---

# Victory Presentation

Victory should feel rewarding.

Display:

Victory message.

Champion shown.

Health remaining.

Possible flavor text.

Example:

"Your champion has proven worthy."

Optional:

Soft glow effect.

---

# Prototype Visual Discipline

If a visual feature does not improve:

Clarity.

Player understanding.

Combat readability.

It should not be added.

---

# MVP Completion Criteria

Arena presentation complete when:

Combat is readable.

Health visible.

Damage visible.

Abilities usable.

Turns understandable.

Victory visible.

Visual polish optional.

Prototype succeeds.
